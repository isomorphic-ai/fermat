# The exponent-thirteen cyclotomic proof

## Source and scope

The source package is
`../fermat-data/flt_11_13_proof_package.zip`.  In accordance with the
package note, the ZIP members are authoritative over the presentation PDF:

- `flt_11_13_cyclotomic_folding.tex` gives the mathematical argument;
- `verify_certificates.py` gives the finite arithmetic certificates;
- `verification-output.txt` records a successful run of that script.

The exponent-thirteen argument has two logically separate parts:

1. prove that the ring of integers of `Q(zeta_13)` has class number one;
2. apply the Lamé–Kummer class-number-one descent.

The uploaded note proves the first part.  Its lines 52--81 explicitly state
the second part as the one substantial imported theorem and give only an
orientation sketch.  In particular, the sketch does not contain the
`1-zeta` valuation analysis, the cyclotomic-unit lemmas, or the decreasing
second-case descent needed for a self-contained formal proof.

The Lean development supplies that boundary with the kernel-checked
[`flt-regular`](https://github.com/leanprover-community/flt-regular)
formalization.  The dependency is pinned to commit
`ff3daecc6c506b3748b28ff7fbe7ce7e6a23e3e5`, the revision compatible with
this project's Lean and Mathlib `v4.30.0`.  Its theorem `flt_regular`
formalizes both Kummer cases; it is not introduced as an axiom.

## The finite class-number certificate

For `K = Q(zeta_13)`, the package uses

- degree `12` and signature `(0, 6)`;
- discriminant `13^11`;
- Minkowski bound `306.415803... < 307`;
- the complete list of possible small prime-ideal norms

  ```text
  (q, residue degree, norm)
  (3,   3,  27)
  (13,  1,  13)
  (53,  1,  53)
  (79,  1,  79)
  (131, 1, 131)
  (157, 1, 157)
  ```

The ramified prime above `13` is generated by `1-zeta_13`.  The five
remaining generators in the ZIP are

```text
1 - zeta^2 - zeta^3              norm 27
1 - zeta + zeta^3                norm 53
1 - zeta - zeta^4                norm 79
1 - zeta - zeta^3                norm 131
1 - zeta + zeta^2 + zeta^4       norm 157
```

The formal certificate uses these same five witness polynomials as
generators and checks integral Bézout identities with `Phi_13`.  Those
identities are a determinant-free form of the ZIP's resultant
certificates: they identify the corresponding Kummer--Dedekind prime ideal
with the principal ideal generated by the witness.  Galois conjugacy then
covers every prime above the same rational prime.  The exact Minkowski
floor and the finite enumeration are supplied by
`FltRegular.SmallNumbers.Thirteen.Thirteen`; unlike the Python audit
script, the Lean bound uses certified rational upper and lower bounds for
`pi` and `sqrt(13^11)`, not floating-point comparison.

The quadratic-period and `Psi_13` identities elsewhere in the note explain
the relationship with the historical exponents five and seven.  The note
itself observes that these identities do not close an elementary
exponent-thirteen descent; they are not logical premises of FLT13.

## Lean theorem map

- `Fermat.Thirteen.Cyclotomic.ringOfIntegers_isPrincipalIdealRing` is the
  class-number-one/PID boundary.
- `Fermat.Thirteen.Cyclotomic.isRegularPrime_thirteen` turns class number
  one into regularity.
- `Fermat.Thirteen.Cyclotomic.holdsAt_thirteen_cyclotomic` applies the
  formal Lamé–Kummer theorem.
- `Fermat.holdsAt_thirteen` is the public project theorem.

## Trust audit

The public theorem depends only on Lean's standard quotient and classical
axioms:

```text
[propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound]
```

There are no project axioms, `sorry`, `admit`, `native_decide`, or unsafe
declarations on the proof path.
